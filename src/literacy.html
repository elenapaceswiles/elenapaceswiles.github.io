Murder or Healthcare?

It depends who you ask. Regardless, prospective mothers have less time to decide.

Although I had two semesters of experience writing investigative stories, this one challenged my own bias the most. Prior to writing this article, I had my own beliefs about abortion and was fairly knowledgeable on the issue. I live in a liberal area and the vast majority of my school community is pro-choice. While my research taught me more about the side I was already familiar with, the most valuable part of the experience was learning from those on the other side. Through this process, I was forced to venture outside of my comfort zone and reach out to dozens of churches and pro-life organizations in the hope someone would speak with me. By including these sources, I was able to balance the perspectives I already had. Since I felt like this was such an important topic, I asked my bilingual classmates to translate my article, so it could reach the whole school.

CULTURE

When Northgate’s fate went south

As a historic mall sits empty, developers and residents debate its future.

Inspired by my investigative piece a year prior, I decided to use my 2022 project to continue pushing for change. I researched an old rundown mall right outside of downtown and learned how it exemplified Durham’s gentrification. This story required me to reconsider my preconceived beliefs. Although I had my own opinions on the property, I spoke with property developers, community members, and city council-members to eliminate bias. After completing this article, I asked my bilingual classmates to translate it into Spanish so it could reach a wider audience.

Early Opportunities Create Unlevel Playing Field

Sophomore Izaiah Jackson will have already completed eight AP classes by the end of the school year. He is one of a handful of sophomores taking AP English III —a class traditionally taken by juniors. His GPA is a 4.6.

I was first introduced to investigative journalism through a 2021 partnership with the Ida B. Wells Society for Investigative Reporting. I published this article under the mentorship of a local journalist. This project sparked my commitment to diversity and love of investigative journalism. It required me to ask difficult questions and ask not just “what?” but “why?” I ensured I understood all sides of the story by learning from district employees in addition to elementary teachers and my peers’ personal experiences.
